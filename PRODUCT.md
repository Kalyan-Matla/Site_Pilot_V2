# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small/medium contractor teams: owners reviewing margins on a laptop, project
managers approving indents from a site office, site engineers logging progress
and attendance on tablets in bright daylight, storekeepers receiving deliveries
at the gate, accountants working ledgers at a desk, vendors checking their POs
and payments. Sessions are task-driven and frequent; bright ambient light and
dusty screens are normal.

## Product Purpose

SitePilot runs the operational spine of a construction business: projects,
tasks, progress reports, materials (indent → PO → GRN → stock → usage), labour
wages, vendor invoices, payments and credit control. Success = a PM trusts the
numbers at a glance and completes any daily action (approve, receive, mark,
pay) in seconds.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, engineered. The feel of a well-machined instrument: quiet
surfaces, exact typography, decisive motion. Reference: Apple.com's restraint —
content-first hierarchy, generous whitespace, translucent chrome, motion that
communicates state (never decoration). Explicitly NOT the reference's assets:
no Apple imagery, fonts stay on the OS system stack.

## Anti-references

- The construction-industry reflex: safety-yellow/orange + navy, hard-hat
  clip-art, "industrial" texture.
- Generic Bootstrap/admin-template dashboards: identical stat cards, side-stripe
  alerts, spinner-centered loading.
- Over-decorated SaaS: gradient text, glass on everything, confetti.

## Design Principles

1. **Numbers first.** The value is the data; chrome defers to it. Tables and
   figures get the contrast budget.
2. **Motion is information.** Every animation marks a state change, feedback,
   or entrance of new data. 150–350ms, ease-out, reduced-motion respected.
3. **One accent, spent on action.** Accent color marks primary actions,
   selection, and live state — never decoration.
4. **Daylight-proof.** High contrast, large targets, legible at arm's length on
   a dusty tablet.
5. **Earned familiarity.** Standard affordances (tables, tabs, dialogs) done
   exceptionally, not reinvented.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, visible focus rings,
`prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation, keyboard-operable
dialogs and forms.
